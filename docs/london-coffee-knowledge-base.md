## Central London

### Monmouth Coffee Company

**Location:** Multiple locations including Borough Market, Covent Garden, and Bermondsey

**What makes it special:**
Monmouth is a London institution, roasting their own beans since 1978. They were pioneers of the specialty coffee movement in the UK.

**Coffee specialty:**
- Single-origin espresso rotated weekly
- Filter coffee from their own roastery
- Signature house blend
- Seasonal guest roasts

**Atmosphere:**
Rustic, minimalist spaces with wooden counters and exposed brick. Often busy with a queue, but worth the wait. Standing room only at most locations.

**Price range:**
- Espresso: £2.50
- Flat white: £3.20
- Filter coffee: £3.00

**Best for:**
Coffee purists, specialty coffee enthusiasts, quick takeaway

**Opening hours:** 8am-6pm Monday-Saturday, varies by location

**Fun fact:** They display their green coffee beans in glass jars, allowing customers to see and smell before purchase.

## Coffee Culture in London

### The London Coffee Scene

London's coffee culture has evolved dramatically since the early 2000s. The city is now home to:
- 200+ independent specialty coffee shops
- 50+ coffee roasteries
- Multiple barista championships held annually
- London Coffee Festival (largest in the UK)
- World-class baristas and roasters

### What Makes London Coffee Special

1. **Diversity**: From Australian-style flat whites to Italian espresso to Scandinavian filter coffee
2. **Competition**: High number of quality cafés drives innovation
3. **Roasteries**: Many shops roast their own beans
4. **Direct Trade**: Ethical sourcing is the norm, not the exception
5. **Barista Skills**: Some of the world's best baristas work in London

### Coffee Trends in London

**Current trends:**
- Cold brew and nitro cold brew
- Single-origin espresso rotations
- Alternative milk options (oat, almond, soy)
- Filter coffee bars with multiple brew methods
- Transparent sourcing and direct trade relationships
- Low-intervention natural wines with coffee
- Coffee cocktails and espresso martinis

## Coffee Terminology Guide

### Drinks

**Espresso**: Single shot of concentrated coffee (25-30ml)

**Americano**: Espresso with hot water added

**Long Black**: Hot water with espresso added on top (Australian/NZ style)

**Flat White**: Double espresso with microfoam milk, originated in Australia/NZ

**Cappuccino**: Espresso with steamed milk and more foam than a flat white

**Latte**: Espresso with lots of steamed milk and minimal foam

**Cortado**: Equal parts espresso and steamed milk

**Macchiato**: Espresso "marked" with a small amount of foamed milk

**Pour Over**: Manual filter coffee method (V60, Chemex, etc.)

**AeroPress**: Manual brewing device creating concentrated coffee

**Cold Brew**: Coffee steeped in cold water for 12-24 hours

---

### Coffee Bean Origins

**Ethiopia**: Birthplace of coffee, fruity and floral notes

**Colombia**: Balanced, sweet, with caramel notes

**Brazil**: Nutty, chocolatey, low acidity

**Kenya**: Bright, acidic, berry-like flavors

**Guatemala**: Complex, cocoa notes, full-bodied

**Costa Rica**: Clean, balanced, honey sweetness

**Sumatra**: Earthy, full-bodied, low acidity

## Coffee Shop Etiquette in London

### Do's
- Order at the counter, don't wait for table service
- Try the house blend or single-origin
- Ask baristas for recommendations
- Take reusable cups for discounts (typically 25p off)
- Tip if service is good (not mandatory)
- Ask about the coffee origin if interested

### Don'ts
- Don't expect free WiFi everywhere (many specialty shops don't offer it)
- Don't occupy tables for hours without ordering more
- Don't ask for "extra hot" milk (destroys the coffee)
- Don't expect flavored syrups (most specialty shops don't do them)
- Don't be offended if laptops aren't welcome during busy times

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the best coffee shop in London?

There's no single "best" - it depends on what you value. For consistency and quality: Monmouth Coffee. For innovation: Workshop Coffee. For atmosphere: Ozone Coffee Roasters.

### Where can I buy London-roasted coffee beans?

Most specialty cafés sell their beans. Top choices: Square Mile, Caravan, Climpson & Sons, Workshop Coffee, Ozone, Volcano.

### Do I need to tip in London coffee shops?

Tipping isn't mandatory but appreciated. Tip jars are usually at the counter. £1 for a great coffee/service is common.

### What's a flat white?

An Australian/New Zealand coffee drink: double espresso with microfoam milk, smaller and stronger than a latte.

### Can I work in London coffee shops?

Many welcome laptop workers, but some don't during busy times. Look for larger cafés with plenty of seating. Be respectful and don't hog tables.

### What's the difference between specialty coffee and regular coffee?

Specialty coffee scores 80+ points on the 100-point Coffee Quality Institute scale. It's higher quality beans, carefully roasted, and skillfully brewed.

### Are there any 24-hour coffee shops in London?

Very few. Most close by 6-7pm. Some central locations may stay open until 9-10pm. BAO Fitzrovia and some chains stay open later.
